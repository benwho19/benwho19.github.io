Hi, I'm Ben. I enjoy working with large, messy datasets to uncover actionable insights from them. I love the lightbulb-moment feeling of connecting the dots and the satisfaction of solving tough technical problems.

A little about me: I live in the Bay Area and was born and raised in San Francisco. In my spare time, I enjoy staying active, spending time with friends and family, and watching TV shows (some of my favorite shows are Arcane, House of the Dragon, and For All Mankind.)

I graduated from UC Berkeley with a degree in Cognitive Science. During my time there, I was introduced to subjects like computer science and data science. I tackled interesting projects such as determining the relationship between U.S. states' crime rates and capital punishment laws.

Afterwards, I found work as a software engineer - but after about a year, I realized that a data role might be better suited for me, for these reasons:

  • I realized that I enjoyed the feeling of discovering more than the feeling of building.
  • Many of the same things that attracted me to software engineering also attract me to data analysis, such as brainstorming creative solutions and utilizing code to solve real-world problems.

So I decided to make the switch. I'm excited for what the future holds and can't wait to dive in.
